Project Scope and Objectives: I took charge of planning and delivering the project's scope and objectives. This tool was seamlessly integrated into the DOT's Truck Eye View Program, contributing to the department's commitment to safety and awareness.

My internship at the Department of Transportation was an enriching experience that not only allowed me to refine my technical skills but also demonstrated the real-world impact of technology on safety and education. Instructor: Object Oriented Programming | NASA MAA Program

During my tenure as an instructor in the NASA MAA (Minority University Research and Education Project - Aerospace Academy) Program, I had the opportunity to share my expertise in Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) with a group of 80 enthusiastic students. Educational Material Development: As part of my responsibilities, I meticulously created slides and coding trivia to supplement the learning experience. These materials not only enhanced the quality of instruction but also provided students with valuable resources for self-study and revision.

Personalized Tutoring: Recognizing that every student has a unique learning curve, I conducted one-on-one tutoring sessions. These sessions proved instrumental in helping individual students overcome challenges and solidify their understanding of fundamental programming concepts, which can be applied across various programming languages.

My experience as an instructor in the NASA MAA Program was not only professionally fulfilling but also personally rewarding. It allowed me to foster the next generation of programmers and share my passion for programming and education.
